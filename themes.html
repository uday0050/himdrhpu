Research Themes

1. Geotechnical, Geological, and Seismic Hazards

Landslides, slope stability, earthquakes, ground improvement, and resilient infrastructure.

2. Geography and Spatial Sciences in DRR

Hazard mapping, land use and land cover studies, spatial planning, remote sensing, GIS applications, and regional vulnerability assessments.

3. Tourism and Cultural Heritage Resilience

Eco-tourism, safe tourism practices, disaster preparedness in tourist hubs, and safeguarding cultural/natural heritage.

4. Community Resilience, Capacity Building, and Human Dimensions

Disaster psychology, trauma management, training and skill development, community preparedness, social equity, migration, rural development, and livelihood adaptation.

5. Law, Governance, and Public Policy

Disaster law, environmental law, institutional frameworks, disaster governance, and mainstreaming DRR into planning and public policy.

6. Economics, Commerce, and Management

Economic impact assessments of disasters, risk financing, insurance mechanisms, resilient supply chains, and post-disaster recovery.

7. Environmental Systems and Ecosystem Resilience

Forest fires, biodiversity conservation, ecosystem-based adaptation, and sustainable resource management.

8. Hydro-Meteorological and Climate-Induced Risks

Floods, cloudbursts, glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs), avalanches, hydrological modelling, and climate projections.

9. Technological Innovations and Engineering Solutions

Remote sensing, GIS, AI/ML applications, IoT-based disaster monitoring, sensor networks, resilient infrastructure design, robotics, communication systems, and smart grids.

10. Mathematical and Statistical Modelling

Quantitative risk modelling, probabilistic hazard analysis, optimization methods, stochastic simulations, and data-driven prediction frameworks.

11. Cross-sectoral Integration for Sustainable Development

Health, education, media and communication, and sustainable policy linkages for resilience infrastructure.
